5 Things to Check Before Signing Your Apartment Lease

May 18, 2026 8 min read By Residia Team

Signing a lease is one of the most important steps in the rental process. It’s a legally binding contract that protects both you and your landlord — but only if you read it carefully.

Many renters skip the fine print and end up surprised by hidden fees, unclear terms, or unexpected restrictions.

1 The Exact Monthly Rent & What It Includes

The monthly rent should be crystal clear in your lease. But here’s what many renters miss: what does that rent actually cover?

Questions to ask:

  • Is water included, or will I be billed separately?
  • What about electricity and gas?
  • Is internet/Wi-Fi provided?
  • Are there building maintenance fees?

Pro Tip:

Get the total monthly cost in writing. A Rp 2 million rent can easily become Rp 2.5 million once you add utilities and parking.

2 Security Deposit Terms & Refund Policy

Most leases require a security deposit — usually 1–2 months’ rent. This money is held by the landlord and should be returned when you move out.

Red Flag:

If the lease says “deposit is non-refundable” or gives the landlord unlimited rights to withhold it, negotiate better terms.

3 Maintenance & Repair Responsibilities

Your AC breaks. The toilet won’t flush. A pipe bursts. Who is responsible for fixing it — you or the landlord?

Landlord Usually Covers:

  • Structural issues
  • Plumbing problems
  • Electrical problems
  • Major appliance repairs

Tenant Usually Covers:

  • Damage caused by guests
  • Light bulb replacement
  • Minor fixes you caused
  • General cleaning

4 Rules & Restrictions That Affect Your Lifestyle

Can you have guests over? Can you own pets? Can you sublet the place? These rules matter because they affect your daily life.

Check for:

  • Pet policy
  • Guest rules
  • Subletting rules
  • Noise restrictions

5 Lease Duration & Early Termination Clause

Most leases in Indonesia are 6 months or 1 year. But what happens if you need to move early due to job changes, family emergency, or other unexpected circumstances?

Pro Tip:

Ask if you can end the lease early with notice, transfer the lease, or pay a fixed penalty.

Final Thoughts: Read Before You Sign

A lease isn’t just a formality — it’s your safety net. Taking 30 minutes to read and understand it now can save you thousands later.

Quick Checklist:

  • Total monthly cost
  • Maintenance responsibility
  • Lease duration & early exit options
  • Security deposit & refund terms
  • House rules
  • Move-in inspection documented
